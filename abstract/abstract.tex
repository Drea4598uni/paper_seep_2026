\begin{abstract}
Accurate prediction of horizontal-axis wind turbine (HAWT) wakes is
essential for wind farm layout optimisation, energy yield estimation and
load assessment. Despite that, it remains a major challenge for affordable
simulation tools. Large Eddy Simulation (LES) reproduces wake physics
reliably but at a prohibitive computational cost, while unsteady
Reynolds-Averaged Navier-Stokes (URANS) systematically
mis-predicts the wake recovery because of the assumptions embedded in the
eddy-viscosity hypothesis. This work proposes a CFD-ML methodology that
defines, rather than post-processes, a machine learning-based turbulence
closure for HAWT wakes for URANS $k$-$\omega$ SST models. From a high-fidelity LES of the NREL-5MW turbine
operating in a turbulent atmospheric boundary layer (ABL), an LES-equivalent
eddy-viscosity field $\nu_{t,eq}$ is extracted and used as the reference
target. A K-means clustering algorithm first segments the flow into
physically distinct regions, and a Multi-Layer Perceptron (MLP) is then trained
to predict the local correction $\Delta\nu_t = \nu_{t,eq} -
\nu_{t,\mathrm{URANS}}$. The resulting closure is embedded directly into a
modified OpenFOAM PIMPLE solver using the $k$-$\omega$ SST URANS model and evaluated in a fully predictive
setting on progressively coarser grids. The trained network reproduces
the LES-equivalent eddy viscosity with a validation coefficient of
determination $R^2 = 0.9934$, against $R^2 = -7.0$ for the baseline RANS
field, and the clustering step is shown to improve the reconstruction
in the near-terrain region. Once embedded in the solver, the ML-based
RANS model consistently outperforms the standard RANS model in both the
near- and far-wake. Most notably, on ultra-coarse grids the model
accurately captures the post-breakdown wake recovery at a computational
cost about $1.25$ times lower than the reference LES, while also
reproducing the rotor forces and power coefficients. These results
indicate that compact, data-driven turbulence closures can deliver
LES-quality wake predictions at a cost compatible with practical wind
farm applications.
\end{abstract}

\keywords{Wind turbine wake, URANS, Machine learning, Turbulence modelling, LES, CFD, Clustering, Neural Networks}
